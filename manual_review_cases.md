# Manual Review Cases

## Purpose

Some customers exhibit conflicting signals and cannot be reliably assigned a retention action through automated segmentation alone. These customers require manual review.

---

## Customer ID: 1001

* High monetary value
* High refund rate

Decision: Retain aggressively and investigate product dissatisfaction.

---

## Customer ID: 1002

* High frequency
* Increasing recency

Decision: Early intervention before the customer becomes at-risk.

---

## Customer ID: 1003

* High web activity
* Low purchasing activity

Decision: Offer conversion-focused promotions.

---

## Customer ID: 1004

* Low frequency
* Very high order value

Decision: Treat as occasional VIP customer.

---

## Customer ID: 1005

* Moderate spending
* High support ticket count

Decision: Resolve service issues before marketing intervention.

---

## Customer ID: 1006

* Strong campaign engagement
* Low purchase activity

Decision: Personalized offers to improve conversion.

---

## Customer ID: 1007

* High spending
* Long inactivity period

Decision: Priority win-back campaign.

---

## Customer ID: 1008

* High frequency
* Rising refund behavior

Decision: Investigate satisfaction issues immediately.

---

## Customer ID: 1009

* Recent purchases
* Low engagement outside transactions

Decision: Monitor behavior and encourage deeper engagement.

---

## Customer ID: 1010

* Medium monetary value
* Mixed engagement signals

Decision: Manual assessment before allocating retention budget.

---

# Summary

These cases demonstrate that RFM metrics alone may not capture the full customer story. Combining spending, engagement, refunds, and support interactions helps produce more accurate retention decisions and reduces the risk of misclassifying valuable customers.
