# Retention Strategy

## Segment 1: Champions

### Customer Behavior

* Recent purchases
* High frequency
* High monetary value
* Strong engagement

### Recommended Action

* VIP rewards program
* Early access to new products
* Exclusive offers

### Expected Business Value

Protects the most valuable customers and maximizes customer lifetime value.

---

## Segment 2: Loyal Customers

### Customer Behavior

* Frequent purchases
* Consistent engagement
* Moderate to high spending

### Recommended Action

* Loyalty points
* Membership upgrades
* Personalized product recommendations

### Expected Business Value

Encourages continued purchasing and increases customer retention.

---

## Segment 3: At-Risk Customers

### Customer Behavior

* Long gap since last purchase
* Low frequency
* Declining engagement

### Recommended Action

* Win-back campaigns
* Personalized discounts
* Reminder emails

### Expected Business Value

Prevents customer loss and recovers potentially valuable customers.

---

## Segment 4: Dormant Customers

### Customer Behavior

* Very high recency
* Minimal recent activity
* Low engagement

### Recommended Action

* Low-cost reactivation campaigns
* Limited-time offers

### Expected Business Value

Provides an opportunity to recover inactive customers without excessive spending.

---

## Segment 5: Discount-Sensitive Customers

### Customer Behavior

* High browsing activity
* Lower purchasing activity
* Responsive to promotions

### Recommended Action

* Targeted discount campaigns
* Coupon-based promotions

### Expected Business Value

Improves conversion rates while controlling marketing spend.

---

## Segment 6: High-Value but Unhappy Customers

### Customer Behavior

* High spending
* Elevated support ticket count
* Higher refund rate

### Recommended Action

* Proactive customer service outreach
* Issue resolution
* Personalized account management

### Expected Business Value

Prevents the loss of valuable customers and protects revenue.

---

# Campaign Budget Prioritization

Assume a limited retention budget of 100 units.

Recommended allocation:

1. High-Value but Unhappy Customers – 35%
2. At-Risk Customers – 25%
3. Champions – 20%
4. Loyal Customers – 10%
5. Discount-Sensitive Customers – 5%
6. Dormant Customers – 5%

## Priority Rationale

High-Value but Unhappy Customers and At-Risk Customers represent the greatest immediate revenue risk. Retaining these groups provides the highest return on retention investment. Champions should also be protected because they contribute a significant share of revenue.
